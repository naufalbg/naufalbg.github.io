Technical Review · Control Engineering · 2024

Advancements in Valve Actuator Technologies for Offshore Platforms and Subsea Installations: A Technical Review

Naufal Bagas Ganefriady

This paper presents a comprehensive technical review of recent advancements in valve actuator technologies as applied to offshore platforms and subsea installations within the oil and gas industry. The study examines four principal actuator types—hydraulic, pneumatic, electric, and electro-hydraulic—with particular emphasis on their suitability under extreme subsea conditions characterised by high hydrostatic pressure, low temperature, corrosive marine environments, and inaccessibility for routine maintenance. Key advancements discussed include the growing adoption of electric actuators in deep-water applications, IIoT integration enabling real-time diagnostics and predictive maintenance, the transition from natural-gas pneumatic actuators to environmentally superior electric alternatives, and the deployment of High Integrity Pressure Protection Systems (HIPPS) alongside Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS). Results indicate that electric actuators present the most favourable profile for subsea and IIoT-integrated deployments, while hydraulic systems retain advantages in applications requiring very high torque at depth.

Keywords: valve actuators · offshore platforms · subsea installations · electric actuators · IIoT · HIPPS · oil and gas automation

Design of a Fault-Tolerant LoRaWAN Communication System for a Mining Early Warning System

Naufal Bagas Ganefriady

This paper proposes the design and implementation of a fault-tolerant LoRaWAN communication architecture specifically engineered for Early Warning Systems (EWS) in mining environments. Mining operations often face challenges related to extreme topographies and signal attenuation, which can disrupt critical safety communications. By implementing a redundant gateway topology and adaptive data rate algorithms, this system ensures high availability and reliable packet delivery for sensor nodes monitoring hazardous conditions. The evaluation demonstrates significant improvements in network resilience, reducing packet loss and latency during simulated node and gateway failures.

Keywords: LoRaWAN · IoT · Mining Safety · Early Warning System · Fault-Tolerant · Wireless Sensor Networks

Technical Analysis · Computer Science

Analysis of Prefix Front Coding

Naufal Bagas Ganefriady

This technical analysis explores the efficiency and implementation mechanics of the Prefix Front Coding algorithm for data compression, specifically focusing on string processing within large datasets. Prefix Front Coding optimizes storage by identifying and compressing common prefixes among sequentially stored strings, which is particularly beneficial for distributed databases and search engine indices. The study analyzes the algorithmic time complexity during encoding and decoding phases, memory overhead, and compression ratios achieved across various data distributions. Results highlight the trade-offs between processing speed and storage efficiency, offering optimization strategies for real-time systems.

Keywords: Data Compression · Prefix Front Coding · Algorithm Optimization · String Processing · Database Systems
